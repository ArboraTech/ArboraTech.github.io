Phase 3

Carbon Nanotubes

Summary

During this phase, we plan on establishing the processes and infrastructure necessary to produce carbon nanotubes from the captured carbon dioxide. To allow for the increased requirement for carbon dioxide, we also plan on expanding our center or opening new carbon capture centers with the goal of capturing more carbon dioxide.

Furthermore, we plan on continuing to work to improve the carbon capture efficiency, the exact method of which depends on whether we build our own systems or partner with Climeworks.

How It Works

There is a large amount of research surrounding the conversion of CO2 into carbon nanotubes (CNTs). One such method uses molten electrolysis to separate CO2 into pure carbon nanotubes and pure oxygen components. The absorption of carbon dioxide into a liquid alkali carbonate creates heat needed for the remaining reaction. In this way, the process is self-heating and continuously renews the electrolyte by continuous addition of CO2. Using this process, 1 ton of CO2 can produce 1 ton of high-quality CNTs. Read more about the process here.

On top of the CNTs produced during this process, the oxygen gas that forms as a byproduct can also be sold to various businesses that require oxygen, producing a completely sustainable process with no negative impact on the environment.
